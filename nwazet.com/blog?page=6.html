  • Netduino Go firmware 4.2.1 - SPI frequency bug

    Secret Labs recently released firmware update 4.2.1 for Netduino Go. While the update introduces bug fixes and improvements over 4.2.0, it also introduces a regression which cuts in half the maximum frequency of the SPI bus and limits it to 10MHz. In turn, this impacts the Nwazet Touch Display and DAQ modules, causing our drivers to hang on the Initialize() call: by default, these drivers are configured to run at 25Mhz and 16Mhz, respectively. Unless required, we recommend staying on firmware 4.2.0 until Secret Labs addresses this issue in a future firmware update. Nwazet customers who have already upgraded to 4.2.1 can either revert to firmware 4.2.0 to regain maximum SPI performance or they can override the default SPI frequency of the Touch Display and DAQ drivers like so: module.Initialize(GoSockets.Socketx, 10000); (10000 kHz == 10MHz). Replace 'Socketx' with the actual socket number connected to the module. The regression and its mitigation are documented in the Netduino community forums here.
