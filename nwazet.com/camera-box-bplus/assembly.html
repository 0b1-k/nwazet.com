Nwazet Pi B+ Camera Box Assembly Instructions

The laser-cut parts of the camera box are protected by an adhesive white or brown paper which needs to be removed before assembly.

Bill of Materials

  • Raspberry Pi model B+
  • Raspberry Pi Camera
  • 6x black matte acrylic plates
  • 3x thin clear acrylic plates
  • 7x 1/4" black screws
  • 1x 3/16" steel screw
  • 4x 7/16" black screws
  • 4x hex nuts
  • 3x 1" M/F hex standoffs
  • 4x 1/4" F/F hex standoffs
  • 4x rubber feet

Optional for wall mounting:

  • wall mount
  • 2x M5 screws
  • 2x M5 nuts

Optional:

  • magnetic lens

Note

Acrylic is a hard and stiff plastic which is sensitive to stress concentrations and shares a certain level of fragility with glass. Acrylic can scratch easily and should be cleaned with a mild solution of dish detergent and warm water. Never use cleaners unsuitable for acrylic. Never use a dry, abrasive cloth.

The matte side of the plates should be on the outside, and the glossy side on the inside.

Here are all the pieces from the bill of materials, before the acrylic pieces have had their protective paper peeled off.

Peel off the paper from both sides of the acrylic plates.

The protective paper or plastic must be removed from both sides of all acrylic parts.

With the Raspberry Pi model B+'s USB ports facing left, insert one 1" male/female standoff into the top-left, top-right, and bottom-right holes in the board, and secure it by screwing a 1/4" female/female standoff under the board. The holes can be a little tight, so you may have to screw the standoffs into them. For the last hole on the bottom-left of the board, screw the 3/16" screw in, then secure it under the board with the last 1/4" standoff.

Locate the transparent part that has two larger holes in it, and orient it exactly like on the photo above. The holes must be offset to the right, and the rectangular hole should be on top.

With the camera's lens facing down, insert the camera's ribbon cable into the T-shaped hole in the transparent part. You can verify that the camera board's components fit exactly into the rectangular hole, like on the photo.

Locate the camera connector on the Raspberry Pi, to the left of the HDMI port and to the right of the Ethernet and USB ports. Very gently pull up both ends of the top part of the connector. It should make a small clicking sound and should move a little around the connector, leaving enough space to easily insert the camera's ribbon cable. Insert the camera's ribbon cable into the camera connector, making sure the contacts face the HDMI port. Holding the cable to the bottom of the connector, and making sure it remains properly aligned, push the top part of the connector back down. The cable should now be securely attached to the Raspberry Pi.

Locate the walls with the HDMI port, and the wall with the Ethernet and USB ports, orient and assemble them like on the photo, then fit them onto the Pi.

Find the two other walls, and assemble them together with the first two, and the Pi.

Putting a rubber band around the walls can help keep the parts together during the rest of the assembly process.

If you are not using the optional wall mount, you can skip this part. If you are, locate the two M5 screws and nuts.

Insert the two M5 screws into the two corresponding holes of the bottom of the case and through the wall mount. The heads of the screws should be on the inside of the box -that is the glossy side of the plate-.

Make sure the wall mount and the bottom plate are properly aligned, then secure the wall mount onto the bottom of the case with the two M5 nuts.

Carefully pick up the pi and four walls that you assembled earlier, then align and assemble them with the bottom of the case.

You may now remove the rubber band, as the walls should be secured in place by the bottom plate.

Carefully turn the assembled pieces upside-down. Use four shorter black screws to secure the Pi to the bottom plate.

Once all screws are in place, turn the case over again. Before you start assembling the camera into the case's lid, you may want to consider applying the optional lens' magnetic ring now, in order to ensure the ring is securely attached. To do so, you may use adhesive tape to remove any dust that may be on the engraved part of the case's lid, where we are going to add the magnetic ring. This will help getting a better adhering surface. Remove the protective stickers from both sides of the magnetic ring, then apply the adhesive side of the ring onto the case's lid around the camera's lens. Press onto the ring, then turn the lid over and press on it against the table to ensure a good fit.

Locate the top plate (the one with the square hole for the camera, and the only one left), and the lens guide (the transparent plate with one large round hole and four smaller screw holes). Align the lens guide with the case's lid, and insert the four longer black screws into both parts.

Use some adhesive tape to secure the screws in place during the rest of the assembly process.

Turn the case's lid over. Locate the transparent part with the L-shaped hole. Put it in place over the four screws like on the photo. Be especially careful about the orientation of this part.

Insert the Pi camera into place, aligned exactly like on the photo.

Add the last transparent plate, that should be already around the camera's ribbon cable as we added it before we secured the camera into the Pi's board. Add the four nuts and screw them in gently. Don't screw the nuts in too tight, as this may break the acrylic plates. The clear acrylic plates should bend a little under the pressure. Screw the nuts enough that they hold the camera firmly to the top black plate, but not to the point where excessive constraints are imposed on the clear plates.

Tuck the camera's ribbon cable into the case, then put the lid in place. Make sure the ribbon cable doesn't get stuck between the walls or one of the standoffs and the lid, then press the lid in place. Start securing the lid in place using the last three short black screws.

Once all the screws are in place, and if you're not adding an optional lens, you are done with the assembly once you have removed the protective sticker on the lens. Congratulations, and enjoy your camera case. If you are adding a lens, please follow along for a few more steps.

If you haven't already applied the optional lens' magnetic ring, now is a great time to do it. You may use adhesive tape to remove any dust that may be on the engraved part of the case's lid, where we are going to add the magnetic ring. This will help getting a better adhering surface. Remove the protective stickers from both sides of the magnetic ring, then apply the adhesive side of the ring onto the case's lid around the camera's lens. Press onto the ring to ensure a good fit.

You can now position the lens on the case. Congratulations, you have completed the assembly of your camera case.
